SVM is a non-parametric supervised machine learning method that was initially created to address binary classification issues. Based on the idea of structural risk minimization (SRM), it maximises and isolates the hyper-plane and the data points closest to the hyper-spectral plane's angle mapper (SAM). It uses a hyper-spectral plane to divide data points into several groups. The use of vectors in this method guarantees that the margin's width will be maximised. Multiple continuous and categorical variables, as well as linear and non-linear samples in many classes, may all be supported by SVM. Support vectors are the training samples or bordering samples that define the SVM's hyper-plane or margin. The polynomial and radial basis function (RBF) kernel has been employed in remote sensing
